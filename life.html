Luke Albert Combs was born on March 2, 1990 in Charlotte, North Carolina until his family relocated to Ashville when he was eight. Singing was always his passion and while at A.C. Reynolds High School he performed at the famous Carnegie Hall in New York City. After high school he attended Appalachian State University in Boone North Carolina. He played his first show during college at the Parthenon Café. He left college just short of graduation to pursue his music career in Nashville, Tennesse

Luke attributes his success to his parents. Luke lives near Nashville with his fiancé Nicole Hocking. At home he enjoys a variety of outdoor activities, but especially likes to hunt. The couple already had a few cats, but recently adopted a sweet black pup named Jojo from a rescue shelter.

In 2020 Luke was on tour in Europe when the pandemic hit. Like many artists his plans came to an abrupt halt. Luke spent his time at home writing the now popular pandemic song called, “Six Feet Apart.” The song’s lyrics are very relatable. It talks about missing loved ones. Luke misses his parents. It talks about what people will do when the pandemic over. Luke wants to eat out and go to a ball game. Finally, the lyrics address the uncertainty of the pandemic. Truthfully no one knows when this will be over.

Luke Albert Combs is a traditional country singer. Many of his fans credit him for preserving classic country music at a time many other artists are moving towards a pop country style. He has won numerous awards including an award at the CMT Music Awards for the breakthrough video of the year for his song Hurricane. In 2019 Luke’s success continued with a nomination at the Grammy Awards for ‘Best New Artist.’ He was also nominated for ‘Video of the Year Award’ at the CMT Music Awards and the won ‘Performance of the Year Award.’ He wrapped up the year by collaborating with Brooks and Dunn on a single titled, “Brand New Man.”
